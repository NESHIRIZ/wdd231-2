The Rich History of Tsumeb

Tsumeb, located in the Oshikoto Region of Namibia, is a town steeped in history and rich mineral heritage. With a population of approximately 27,000 people, Tsumeb has evolved from a small mining settlement into a vibrant commercial and cultural hub in northern Namibia.

1893

Discovery & Foundation

Tsumeb was founded following the discovery of rich copper deposits in the area. German prospectors and miners established the settlement as a mining outpost, recognizing the economic potential of the mineral-rich region.

1906

Mining Expansion

The Tsumeb Corporation Limited was established to formally develop and manage copper mining operations. This marked the beginning of large-scale mineral extraction and the town's transformation into a major mining center.

1920s-1960s

Golden Era of Mining

Tsumeb became one of Africa's most important mining centers, producing copper, lead, zinc, and other precious metals. The prosperity attracted workers and businesses from across the region, transforming Tsumeb into a thriving cosmopolitan town.

1990

Independence & Transition

Following Namibia's independence from South Africa, Tsumeb underwent significant economic and social changes. The town began diversifying its economy beyond mining, developing retail, hospitality, and service sectors.

2000s-Present

Modern Development

Today, Tsumeb is a modern commercial center with a diverse economy. While mining remains important, the town has developed strong retail, hospitality, agriculture, and service sectors, making it a key economic hub in northern Namibia.
